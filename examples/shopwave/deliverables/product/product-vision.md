## Executive Summary

ShopWave is an independent online store connecting artisans with conscious consumers. We specialize in handcrafted home goods, focusing on sustainability, transparency, and direct relationships between makers and buyers.

## Product Vision Statement

"To empower artisans to reach global customers while providing unique, sustainably-sourced home goods to conscious consumers."

## Target Audience

### Primary Customer Persona

**Name**: Emma
**Demographics**: 32 years old, urban professional, $75K income
**Goals**: Fill home with unique, meaningful pieces; support independent makers
**Pain Points**: Mass-produced goods lack character; hard to find quality artisans
**How ShopWave Helps**: Curated selection with artisan stories, transparent sourcing

### Secondary Customer Persona

**Name**: Marcus
**Demographics**: 28 years old, minimalist lifestyle, values sustainability
**Goals**: Buy less, buy better; reduce environmental impact
**Pain Points**: Difficulty verifying sustainability claims
**How ShopWave Helps**: Verified sustainable materials, carbon-neutral shipping option

## Core Value Proposition

### Key Benefits

1. **Unique Products**: Handcrafted items you won't find in mass retail
2. **Artisan Stories**: Connect with the makers behind each product
3. **Sustainable**: Transparent materials and shipping impact
4. **Direct Relationship**: Support independent creators directly

## Competitive Landscape

### Market Analysis

**Market Size**: $10B home goods e-commerce market, growing 15% YoY

**Key Competitors**:
- **Etsy**: Strengths (large selection, established), Weaknesses (overwhelming, mixed quality)
- **Amazon Handmade**: Strengths (fast shipping), Weaknesses (not truly independent)
- **West Elm**: Strengths (design), Weaknesses (expensive, mass-produced)

**Our Competitive Advantage**:
- Authentic artisan stories and relationships
- Verified sustainable materials
- Curated quality over quantity
- Direct support of independent makers

## Release Roadmap

## Constraints & Assumptions

### Constraints
- **Technical**: Must use React and Node.js (team expertise)
- **Business**: $50K development budget, 4-month timeline
- **Regulatory**: PCI DSS compliance for payments

### Assumptions
- Assumption 1: Artisans can provide product photos and descriptions
- Assumption 2: Stripe is available for payment processing
- Assumption 3: Customers have modern browsers
- Assumption 4: Shipping partners provide reliable APIs

## Risk Factors

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|--------|------------|
| Stripe integration delays | Medium | High | Early integration, fallback options |
| Artisan onboarding slower | Medium | Medium | Pre-recruit artisans, simplify process |
| Performance issues | Medium | High | CDN, lazy loading, image optimization |
| Scope creep | High | Medium | Strict change control, MVP focus |
